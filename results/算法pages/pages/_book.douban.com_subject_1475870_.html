The book teaches students a range of design and analysis techniques for problems that arise in computing applications. August 6, 2009 Author, Jon Kleinberg, was recently cited in the New York Times for his statistical analysis research in the Internet age.

    6.7 Sequence Alignment in Linear Space via Divide and Conquer
    The problem: Pure DP approach takes O(mn) space and time, too much for biological sequences (100,000 sequence --> 10 G array)
    Solution: an enhancement of the algorithm which runs in O(mn) time and O(m + n) space.
    think the aligment table as a graph
    Notice that in DP, in order to know the score (f(i, j)) of an alignment (i, j), only need to look at the previous two columns in the table.
    DEF - f(i, j) = length of shortest path from (0,0) to (i, j)
    Can compute f(*, j) for any j in O(mn) time and O(m + n) space
    DEF - g(i, j) = length of shortest path from (i, j) to (m, n)
    Can compute g(*, j) for any j in O(mn) time and O(m + n) space
    OBSERVATION1: shortest path that uses (i, j) is f(i, j) + g(i, j)
    OBSERVATION2: let q be an index that minimizes f(q, n/2) + q(n/2), then the shortes path from (0, 0) to (m, n) uses (q, n/2)
    DIVIDE AND CONQUER:
    DIVIDE: find index q that minimizees f(q, n/2) + g(q, n/2) using DP
    (align xq and yn/2)
    CONQUER: recursively comute optimal alignment in each piece
    demo: http://drp.id.au/align/2d/AlignDemo.shtml (Hirschberg)
    推荐

    4.2 minimize maximum lateness - EXCHANGE ARGUMENT
    algorithm: earliest deadline first;
    prove:
    def - inversion
    i scheduled before j but di > dj
    1. all schedules with no inversions and no idle time have the same maximum lateness
    only possible variation of job scheduling = when jobs have the same deadline
    in this situation,
    max lateness = the lateness of the last job = deadline - finish time <-- not effected by the order of the jobs
    proved
    2. there is an optimal schedule that has no inversion and no idle time
    prove by proving:
    a). if O has an inversion, then there is a pair of jobs such that j is scheduled immediately after i and has dj < di
    b). after swapping i and j we get a schedule with one less inversion, the new swapped schedule has a maximum lateness no larger than that of O
    proved
    3. the schedule A produced by the greedy algorithm has optimal maximum lateness L.
    prove:
    from 2: an scheduling that has no inversion and idle time is OPT
    from 1: every schedule which has no inversion and idle time has the same max lateness
    1 & 2 --> the schedule A is of OPT
    algorithm then proved to produce optimal sol.
    NOTE: the optimality of the algor partially depends on the fact that all schedules have the same starting time;
    NOTE: max total lateness --> NP-hard
    推荐
